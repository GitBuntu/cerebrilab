# Governance Comparison Table  
NIST AI RMF vs OECD AI Principles vs Microsoft Responsible AI Standard  
(With Authoritative References)

| Dimension | NIST AI RMF | OECD AI Principles | Microsoft Responsible AI Standard |
|----------|--------------|--------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Primary Reference** | NIST AI Risk Management Framework (2023) — https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework | OECD AI Principles (2019) — https://oecd.ai/en/ai-principles | Microsoft Responsible AI Standard v2 — https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai/responsible-ai |
| **Purpose** | Provide a voluntary, risk‑based framework for trustworthy AI | Establish global norms for responsible AI across governments | Operationalize responsible AI principles into engineering requirements |
| **Scope** | U.S. NIST-led, multi‑stakeholder, cross‑industry | International, intergovernmental (42+ countries) | Corporate governance + engineering implementation |
| **Core Focus** | Risk management: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage | Human rights, fairness, transparency, accountability | Fairness, Reliability & Safety, Privacy & Security, Inclusiveness, Transparency, Accountability |
| **Primary Orientation** | Technical + organizational risk controls | Policy + societal values | Engineering + product lifecycle controls |
| **Lifecycle Coverage** | Full lifecycle: design → development → deployment → monitoring | High-level principles applicable across lifecycle | Detailed lifecycle requirements with checkpoints and documentation |
| **Risk Approach** | Structured risk identification, measurement, mitigation | Encourage risk assessment and continuous monitoring | Mandatory mitigations, guardrails, and human oversight |
| **Documentation Requirements** | Encourages model cards, data documentation | Encourages transparency but not prescriptive | Requires transparency notes, impact assessments, model documentation |
| **Human Oversight** | Recommended as part of governance | Required for accountability | Explicitly required with defined roles and escalation paths |
| **Regulatory Alignment** | Interoperable with global frameworks | Forms a global baseline | Aligns with internal compliance + external regulations |
